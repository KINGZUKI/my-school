On Wednesday, pupils and staff of Castle Academy celebrated the King' coronation in style! Celebrations began at lunch time with Aspens providing a roya menu of a British Roast Dinner and a King's Trifle. This was followed in the afternoon with the children enjoying 'coronation cakes', which they ate together 'tea party style' in their year groups. It was then time for the whole school to go into the hall, where our Castle Academy choir sang the song 'King's Coronation' to everyone. Finally, it was time for everyone to enjoy a theatrical performance of the classic British novel, The Jungle Book, performed for us by M&M Productions. Everyone had a fantastic afternoon with a celebration to remember.

Last term our school council asked all of the children in school how they would like to find out more about our school governors. Our children responded by voting for the opportunity to meet governors and ask them questions about their role. Last Friday, that is exactly what happened as we were visited by David Hood, our Chair of Governors.David visited classes in Reception, Year 2, Year 3 and Year 5. The children were all excited to have David visit their classroom and were very welcoming. All classes had worked hard to prepare questions to ask David about his role as Chair of Governors, what the job entails, what governors are responsible for in school, how he finds out how the school is performing, and how he and the rest of the governing board support Mr Lugg and Mrs McIntyre in continuing to take the school from strength to strength. Dahl class (Year 3), led by Ugnius, did a particularly good job as they organised their questions into a PowerPoint for David to answer.

A royal assembly...

Today we had a whole school assembly informing the children about the coronation. Prior to the assembly, Mr Wieremiej asked each year group to research a question linked to the coronation, and then produce a couple of PowerPoint slides that explain the answers to the questions. Mr Wieremiej then combined the slides into one PowerPoint presentation that was shared in this morning's assembly.

The questions the children were asked to research about King Charles and his coronation were:

  • Early Years: Who is in his family?
  • Year 1: What was his childhood like?
  • Year 2: What education did he receive?
  • Year 3: What was his life like in the Navy and Air Force?
  • Year 4: What charity work has he done?
  • Year 5: What are his official duties?
  • Year 6: What is a coronation? Why does it happen?
  • Caterpillars: What events are taking place around the UK for the coronation?
